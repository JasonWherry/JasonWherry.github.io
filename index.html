SamplingApp ~ OpenSource Personal Project

I first created the SamplingApp because I wanted play around with statistics and start my own interdisciplinary project. Additionally, my Grand Father is a statistician, although he wears many other hats too, and I wanted to bond with him over this as he came to me with the idea.

The goal of random sampling is to show that the average performance of a group (randomly sampled) is an unbiased estimate of the average performance of the entire population. To demonstrate the effectiveness, histograms are plotted which show the variance between different sample sizes. When the size of the randomly sampled group grows, we observe an upward trend towards the average performance of the entire population. In other words, the variance of the averages' changes, depending on the size of the randomly sampled group.

What I mean is, musicians understand how ebb and flow work together, processing the elements of music. If we think about the ocean, the water displays this with its tides: the ebb is the water retracting from the shore, back into the ocean while the flow is the water arriving onto the shore. Two distinct phases that can be practiced, as it is learned through repetition.
